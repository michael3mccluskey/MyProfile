Project Idea

Overview

My project idea is to develop an app that can be used in movie theatres by movie theatre staff that displays all relevant cinema information. The app will focus on optimising cinema cleaning time by displaying exact finish times and how long staff have to clean a particular cinema.

Motivation

I have had experience working in more than one movie theatre, these theatres have changed and evolved through the years to improve the guest experience. Many movie theatres have implemented their own apps for their customers, these apps show customers all available movies on site or all movies playing in the country.
Customers have access to candy bar items via their phone, where they can be delivered to their homes in just a few clicks. However movie theatre staff have implemented very little IT into their work, cinema cleans are deciphered from a printed grid of sessions times. This print out has very little information
which can make cleaning with as little time wasted between sessions difficult.

Description

The app would be for use by cinema staff instead of a printed copy of the day’s sessions. Needless to say the app needs to be able to complete the same job as the printout. It should be possible for the app and cinema planning software to connect in a way it is at least possible import data from cinema software onto the app
removing the need to manually enter new data everyday and the need to create a new input method. The app being the same as the printout should display all movie start times, movie end times, which cinema the movie is playing in and basic movie information (rating). This is the information the printout contains,
however the printout is static, and the information is displayed based on cinema number. The app should be able to sort the order of movies based on time for example the app should list cinema 1 which comes out at 12:00 and then show cinema 6 which come out at 12:10 instead of showing the next session for cinema 1 which is 12:30.
The app should also take the time between the next cinema clean and display it as cleaning time. Cleaning time is important because its not just the time between sessions for one cinema but the time available before another cinema is available to clean. The app should also have the ability to display the amount of tickets
purchased for a particular session. This information is important because it allows cinema staff to make more informed decisions. If a session has a small amount of people in it then less floor staff need to be allocated to clean that cinema meaning they are available to assist in candy bar or complete other duties.
The same is true if there is a full session more staff will be allocated to clean the cinema, staff will know to bring extra bins and rubbish bags which will save time walking back and forth the long cinema halls. The number of people per session will also help cinema workers to allocate certain cinemas during busy times.
When a cinema is fully booked cinema workers will physically walk in the cinema and help direct people to their seats. This process improves guest experience as everyone sits in the seat they paid for and less people have to leave the cinema searching for a staff member to resolve a dispute.
Another feature the app should have is an alert or message that pops up to remind cinema workers to check a cinema 20 minutes after the session has started. This message will prompt cinema workers to complete a cinema check, ensuring that the session is tunning smoothly and everyone is comfortable.
With the printout it is difficult to determine when to conduct a cinema check. Finally the app should have the ability to mark certain sessions, or cinemas, this is to keep track of where bins are placed, what sessions have been checked and what sessions have been cleaned and by who.

Tools & Technologies

Movie theatres like most modern businesses have access to tablets. I believe android is the best way to go with developing the app, it’s overall easier to set up and use while being more accessible than IOS. I believe the app can be developed using a simple machine like the ordinary laptops we use for class.
The software that could be used to develop the app would be android studio. Android studio is an extremely popular development tool used by android developers it is also free to download, comparatively simple to use and was created and currently supported by Google. It may also be appropriate to use AVD
Manager to emulate the running of android apps on a computer for easier testing.

Skills required

The final project when completed should be an android app that can be downloaded and installed on an android device. Most android apps are written in Java so having someone who is proficient in coding Java would be necessary. The app would take information from an existing source, so no new data base system is required.
The app does not require any specialised hardware, so its development is quite possible. Many of the listed features aren’t too complicated and should be doable by someone with the relevant coding knowledge.

Outcome

If the project is successful and a working app is produced I believe it will see use by cinema staff where it can be further tested and updated. If the app does in fact prove to be helpful it may see use by many different cinemas. Hopefully it will increase cinema efficiency,
make lives easier for cinema staff and create a more enjoyable experience for cinema goers. At the very least it should replace the paper printout an reduce the environmental impact cinemas make.
